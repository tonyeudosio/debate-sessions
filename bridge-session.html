Session 1 · Engineering English

How They Built the Brooklyn Bridge

Listening for detail + guided speaking · TED-Ed, ~5 minutes

Before You Watch

suspension bridge — a bridge hanging from cables
cable — a very strong thick wire/rope
to collapse — to fall down suddenly
to snap — to break suddenly
caisson — a watertight chamber used underwater
span — the distance a bridge covers
Read these six words once before watching. You don't need to memorize them — just recognise them when you hear them. This is the only "preparation"; everything else comes from listening.

First Watch — Just the Gist

If the video doesn't load above, watch it directly on YouTube →

Quick check

In one sentence, in your own words: what is this video about?

No pressure here — this first answer is just to confirm you have the general idea. You already know you're good at this part.

Second Watch — Hunting for Details

Now watch again (or in short chunks, pausing where you need to). This time the goal is specific facts. It's completely fine to replay a section two or three times — that's not cheating, that's exactly how this skill is built.

Detail · Names

1. Who proposed building the bridge?

Detail · Cause

2. Why were officials skeptical at first?

Detail · Open answer

3. What problem happened to John Roebling himself during the project? (Listen for a name + an accident.)

Don't worry about perfect grammar here — focus on getting the fact right. We'll go over it together live.

Detail · Numbers

4. Try to catch ONE number from the video (length, height, year, number of workers — anything). Write the number and what it refers to.

Speaking — Using What You Heard

These tasks reuse the vocabulary and facts from the video, so you're not inventing language from nothing — you're recycling what you just heard. Speak for 1–2 minutes on each; I'll listen and we'll talk, not "correct" in real time.

Task A · Retelling (easiest)

Retell the story of the Brooklyn Bridge in your own words, as if explaining it to a colleague who has never heard of it. Use at least three words from the vocabulary list.

Tip: start with "It was built in the 19th century, when..." — you don't need to remember every detail, just the shape of the story.

Task B · Personal connection

Do you know a famous bridge, building or structure in Spain or elsewhere? Describe it briefly — where it is, why it's important, what's interesting about it.

This is the kind of question that often appears in real B2 speaking exams — describing something familiar.

Task C · Engineer's angle

As an engineer, what part of this project do you imagine was the most technically difficult? Why?

There's no wrong answer — this is your professional intuition speaking, not a test of facts.
