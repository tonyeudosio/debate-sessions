1. Inversion

PatternUse + Example
Negative adverbialsNever, Rarely, Seldom, Little, Hardly/Scarcely/No sooner + aux + subject → Never have I seen such arrogance.
No sooner... than / Hardly... whenNo sooner had he sat down than the phone rang.
Conditional inversion (no if)Had / Were / Should + subject → Had I known, I would have acted differently.
So + adj / Such + beSo great was her surprise that she couldn't speak. / Such was the silence that we could hear the clock.
Only after / Only when / Not untilOnly when she left did he realise how much he loved her.
Locative adverbials (literary)Down came the rain. / On the hill stood an ancient castle.

2. Cleft Sentences

TypeFormula + Example
It-cleftIt + be + emphasised element + that/who + clause → It was John who broke the window.
What-cleftWhat + clause + be + emphasised element → What I need is a holiday.
All-cleftAll + clause + be (+ infinitive) → All she wants is to be heard.
⚠️ Key point: cleft sentences don't add new information — they re-package a known idea to emphasise one part of it.

3. Mixed Conditionals

TypeFormula + Example
Past condition → Present resultIf + past perfect, ...would + infinitive → If she had studied medicine, she would be a doctor now.
Present/general condition → Past resultIf + past simple, ...would have + p.p. → If he weren't so stubborn, he wouldn't have lost the deal.

4. Participle Clauses

TypeFormula + Example
Present participle (-ing)Active / cause → Realising his mistake, he apologised at once.
Perfect participleHaving + p.p. (earlier action) → Having finished his homework, he watched TV.
Past participle (-ed)Reduced passive relative clause → The man arrested last night is now in custody.
Negative formNot + participle → Not knowing what to say, she remained silent.

5. Subjunctive Mood

PatternFormula + Example
Formal that-clause subjunctiveverb (recommend / insist / demand / suggest / propose) + that + subject + base form → The judge demanded that the witness tell the truth.
it is + adj. + thatessential / vital / imperative / crucial + that + base form → It is essential that he be informed.
Were-subjunctiveIf I were... / as if / as though + were → If I were you, I'd accept. / He talks as if he were the boss.

6a. Passive Voice — Personal & Impersonal Reporting

Impersonal Passive
It + passive reporting verb + that + clause
It is said that the minister lied.  /  It was believed that she had left.
👉 'It' is a dummy subject; the real information sits in the that-clause, which keeps its own tense.
Personal Passive
Subject (from the that-clause) + passive reporting verb + to-infinitive
The minister is said to have lied.  /  She was believed to have left.
👉 Use to + have + past participle whenever the that-clause action happened before the reporting verb.
ActivePersonal PassiveImpersonal Passive
People say the minister lied.The minister is said to have lied.It is said that the minister lied.
Many believe the painting is a forgery.The painting is believed to be a forgery.It is believed that the painting is a forgery.
Investigators reported the fire had started accidentally.The fire was reported to have started accidentally.It was reported that the fire had started accidentally.
⚠️ Common reporting verbs that take this pattern: say, believe, think, claim, consider, expect, know, report, allege, feel, understand, assume, rumour (be rumoured to).

6b. Causative Passive — have / get something done

PatternUse + Example
have + object + past participleArrange for someone else to do something → I had my car repaired yesterday.
get + object + past participleSlightly more informal; also used for unfortunate events → She got her bag stolen on the bus.
have/get something done (negative event)Something happens to you, with no agent intended → They had their house broken into.

6c. Passive Gerund & Passive Infinitive

PatternUse + Example
Passive gerundbeing + p.p., after verbs like hate, like, remember, resent, mind → He hates being interrupted. / She remembers being praised.
Passive infinitive(to) be + p.p., after want, need, expect, would like → He wants the document translated. / I need this checked.
need / want + -ing (= passive meaning)This essay needs checking. (= needs to be checked)

6d. Passive with Two Objects

give / send / show / offer / tell / promise + indirect object + direct object → either object can become the passive subject
ActivePassive (preferred — indirect object as subject)
They gave her an award.She was given an award.
The company offered him a promotion.He was offered a promotion.
They sent the shareholders a report.The shareholders were sent a report.

6e. Formal / Stative Passive Expressions

  • be held / deemed / regarded + to-infinitive or as → He is held to be responsible. / It is regarded as a masterpiece.
  • be widely considered / generally acknowledged + to-infinitive → She is widely considered to be the finest violinist of her generation.
  • be rumoured / purported + to-infinitive → The deal is rumoured to be worth millions.

6f. Common Passive Pitfalls

⚠️ Intransitive verbs (happen, arrive, occur, exist, disappear) cannot be made passive: ❌ "The accident was happened". ⚠️ Don't confuse the impersonal passive (It is said that...) with a simple passive sentence — the impersonal pattern always needs the dummy "It" + that-clause.
