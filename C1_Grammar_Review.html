1. Advanced Verb Tenses & Inversion

StructureUse + Example
Future in the PastAn event planned in the past that did or did not happen → The committee was to have met yesterday, but it was postponed.
Future Perfect ContinuousProjecting duration forward to a specific point → By next month, I will have been living here for ten years.
Negative InversionEmphatic structure after initial negative adverbs → Seldom have we witnessed such an exceptional performance.
Past Perfect ContinuousDuration highlighted right before a past event → He had been working for hours when the power went out.

3. Key C1 Phrasal Verbs for CAE

Phrasal VerbMeaning + Example
bring aboutcause to happen → The new layout will bring about dramatic improvements.
get round tofinally find time to do something → I haven't got round to replying to his email yet.
catch onbecome popular / understand → The new fashion trend is slowly starting to catch on.
come up againstencounter an obstacle → We came up against unexpected bureaucratic problems.
crop upappear unexpectedly → Several unforeseen issues cropped up during the project.
fall back onuse as a last resort → When the funding stopped, they had to fall back on their savings.
make up forcompensate for → Her enthusiasm more than makes up for her lack of experience.
put down toattribute to → The flight cancellation was put down to adverse weather conditions.
take todevelop a habit / start liking → He has taken to drinking green tea instead of coffee lately.
gloss overavoid detailing a problem → The report completely glossed over the real causes of the crisis.

4a. Urgency & Complex Preferences

StructureUse + Example
would rather + past perfectPast preference about someone else's action → I'd rather you hadn't told her yet.
it is high time + past simpleUrgent advice / complaint about the present → It is high time the government acted.
lest + subjunctiveTo avoid a specific risk → He took notes lest he should forget the main points.

4b. Advanced Conditionals: Inverted & Mixed

StructureUse + Example
Inverted Third ConditionalOmission of "if" in formal past contexts → Had I known, I would have intervened.
Inverted First ConditionalFormal future contingency → Should you require assistance, do not hesitate to ask.
Mixed Conditional (Past/Pres)Past action with a present result → If you had slept more, you wouldn't feel tired now.

4c. Sophisticated Concession Linkers

LinkerExample
much as + clauseStrong concession meaning 'although' → Much as I like him, I disagree with his idea.
adj / adv + as / though + clauseEmphatic structure → Difficult though it was, they managed to complete the task.
notwithstanding + nounFormal contrast → Notwithstanding the heavy rain, the football match continued.
be that as it mayConceding a point but moving past it → Be that as it may, we must look at the facts.

4d. Advanced Passives & Cleft Emphatic Sentences

It-Cleft Structures
It + be + emphasized focus + that / who + relative clause
It was the director who made the ultimate decision, not the supervisor.
👉 Shifts absolute emphasis directly onto a specific agent or point.
Wh-Cleft / Pseudo-Cleft
What + clause + be + noun phrase / infinitive phrase
What the target demographic genuinely values is transparency.
👉 Packs structural momentum into the sentence by leading with a noun clause.
Standard ActiveIt-Cleft FormatWh-Cleft Format
The music woke me up.It was the music that woke me up.What woke me up was the music.
I need a long holiday.It is a long holiday that I need.What I need is a long holiday.
