Before You Watch

cacao bean — the seed chocolate comes from
pod — the fruit that holds the beans
to roast — to heat something (like coffee or nuts)
to grind — to crush into very small pieces
liquid / paste — not solid, soft or runny
to wrap / package — to put in paper or plastic for selling
Six simple words, that's all. Read them once, then forget about them — your job while watching is just to enjoy the video and recognise these words when they appear.

Speaking — Talking About Chocolate

Easy, personal topic — you already know a lot of this vocabulary in real life, you're just saying it in English. Speak for 1 minute on each; I'll listen and we'll chat afterwards, not interrupt to correct.

Task A · Simple retelling

In a few simple sentences, explain the steps from "bean" to "chocolate bar" — just the basic order, nothing fancy.

Sentence starter: "First, the beans are picked from the pod. Then..."

Task B · Personal connection

Do you like chocolate? What's your favourite type (dark, milk, white)? Is there a chocolate or sweet that reminds you of being a child?

Sentence starter: "I really like... because..." / "When I was a child, I used to eat..."

Task C · A little opinion

Do you think it's strange that something so simple (a bean) becomes something so popular all over the world? Why do you think people love chocolate so much?

Sentence starter: "I think people love chocolate because..."
